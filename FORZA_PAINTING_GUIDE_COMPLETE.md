# Complete Forza Horizon 5 Painting & Special Colors Guide

While basic colors are easy to apply, mastering Forza Horizon 5's advanced painting mechanics—specifically **Two-Tone**, **Metal Flake**, and the **Transparent Vinyl Method**—allows you to create OEM-accurate hypercar paints, deep candy coats, and tinted carbon fiber.

## The Basics: Understanding HSB
Forza uses the **HSB (Hue, Saturation, Brightness)** system on a scale of `0.00` to `1.00`.
- **Hue (H):** The actual color (0.00 is Red, moving through the rainbow back to 1.00 Red).
- **Saturation (S):** How intense the color is (0.00 is grayscale/white, 1.00 is pure color).
- **Brightness (B):** How light or dark the color is (0.00 is pitch black, 1.00 is fully bright).

*Pro-Tip: Press `X` (on controller) or `Space` (on keyboard) when hovering over a color to open the Advanced Fine-Tune menu where you can input exact HSB values.*

## 1. Pearlescent & Color-Shift (Two-Tone Paints)
Two-Tone paints transition between two different colors depending on the angle of the light and the camera. This is how you create famous "color-shift" paints like TVR's Reflex Spice, Nissan's Midnight Purple, or Ford's Mystichrome.

**The Method:**
1. Go to the **Special Colors** tab.
2. Select **Two-Tone Polished** (for a glossy finish) or **Two-Tone Semigloss/Matte**.
3. Press `X` to Fine-Tune.
4. **Base Color (Lowlight):** This is the core color of the car in the shadows. Set this to a darker, richer color (Low Brightness).
5. **Highlight Color:** This is the color that catches the sun. Set this to a bright, highly saturated contrasting color.

**Example: Nissan Midnight Purple III**
- **Base Color:** `H: 0.65 | S: 1.00 | B: 0.25` (Deep Navy/Purple)
- **Highlight Color:** `H: 0.08 | S: 0.90 | B: 0.60` (Vibrant Copper/Orange)

**Example: Ford Mystichrome**
- **Base Color:** `H: 0.56 | S: 1.00 | B: 0.40` (Deep Blue)
- **Highlight Color:** `H: 0.76 | S: 1.00 | B: 0.55` (Vibrant Purple)

## 2. Candy Coats & Deep Metallics (Metal Flake)
Metal Flake paint consists of a solid base coat with tiny metallic flakes suspended in clear coat. This is perfect for creating deep "Candy Apple" hot rod paints, bass boat paints, or standard OEM metallics.

**The Method:**
1. Go to the **Special Colors** tab and select **Metal Flake**.
2. Press `X` to Fine-Tune.
3. **Base Color:** The underlying paint. For a "Candy" look, make this very dark.
4. **Flake Color:** The metallic sparkle. Make this the same Hue as the base, but with maximum Brightness and Saturation.

**Example: Deep Candy Apple Red**
- **Base Color:** `H: 0.00 | S: 1.00 | B: 0.20` (Dark Maroon)
- **Flake Color:** `H: 0.00 | S: 1.00 | B: 1.00` (Bright Red)
*Result: A paint that looks almost black in the shade but explodes into sparkling ruby red in the sunlight.*

---

## 3. Anodized Metals & Colored Chrome
You can tint the highly reflective metals in the game to create custom anodized aluminum, colored chrome, or realistic brass and copper.

**The Method:**
1. Go to the **Special Colors** tab.
2. Select **Chrome**, **Polished Aluminum**, or **Brushed Aluminum**.
3. Press `X` to tint the metal.

**Example: Anodized Blue (Great for race car fittings/wheels)**
- **Material:** Polished Aluminum
- **Tint:** `H: 0.60 | S: 0.85 | B: 0.90`

**Example: Realistic Bronze/Gold**
- **Material:** Semigloss Brass or Brushed Aluminum
- **Tint:** `H: 0.12 | S: 0.65 | B: 0.75`

## 4. Tinted Carbon Fiber (The Transparent Vinyl Method)
Many modern hypercars (like the Pagani Huayra or McLaren Senna) feature exposed, colored carbon fiber weaves. You cannot tint carbon fiber directly in the paint menu, but you can achieve this using the Vinyl editor.

**The Method:**
1. Paint the entire car in **Carbon Fiber** (Polished or Matte) from the Special Colors tab.
2. Exit the paint booth and go to **Apply Decals / Vinyls**.
3. Add a basic **Square** vinyl shape to the top, sides, and back of the car.
4. Scale the squares up massively until they completely cover the entire vehicle.
5. Select the vinyls and change their color to your desired tint (e.g., Blood Red or Sapphire Blue).
6. **The Secret Step:** Change the **Transparency (Opacity)** of the vinyls to around **15% - 30%**.

*Result: The color acts as a transparent clear-coat, allowing the carbon fiber weave to perfectly shine through from underneath, giving you beautiful colored carbon.*

---

## 5. Faux Custom Decal Finishes (Matte/Gloss Contrast)
In FH5, vinyls (decals) automatically match the glossiness of the paint underneath them. However, you can invert this to have glossy decals on a matte car, or matte decals on a glossy car, creating incredible stealth/ghost patterns.

**The Method:**
1. Paint the car in **Matte** or **Semigloss** black.
2. Apply your decals (e.g., racing stripes or logos) in the same black color.
3. While selecting the decals, look for the prompt to **Toggle Vinyl Material** (usually pressing `Y` on controller or viewing vinyl options).
4. Set the vinyl to be Glossy while the car remains Matte.

*Result: A stealthy "Batman" aesthetic where the logos are only visible when the light reflects off the glossy parts against the matte body.*

## General Painting Tips
- **Wheels:** When painting wheels, "Advanced" painting allows you to paint the inner barrel, the spokes, and the lip separately.
- **Brake Calipers:** Calipers do not support special paints like Two-Tone or Metal flake natively, but you can use normal HSB to match the highlight hue of your car's body.
- **Lighting:** Always take your car outside to the Horizon Festival during the daytime to check the paint. The lighting inside the garage/autoshow is artificial and will make colors look slightly washed out compared to natural sunlight.
